Sketching & Wireframing

Creating the skeleton

Once I have a good idea of what the problem is, our data, and a good idea of the competitive landscape I begin to sketch ideas and experiment with different options. During this time I try to really focus on our target user and put myself in their shoes, what would they want, what would they expect the interface to do. I firmly believe the best way to improve your customers experience is through Empathy. You need to understand them before you can help them.

Hi-Fi Prototypes

Rapid prototyping to prove concepts

I try to get to testing my ideas as soon as possible, the faster we fail the faster we improve.

I began testing the signin dialog using HTML/CSS, Javascript, and Screen shots of the design comps. Once I have things in place I can test out flows and make sure it meets our users needs.

Implimentation | Testing | Iteration

Test and Test some more

Once the designs have been implimented the real fun begins. In startups funds can be limited so I had to get really creative. I using the metrics defined earlier along with creating our personas in Google Analytics I am able to look at how users are using the site and find pain points.

In a startup you have to be very creative with resources. You may not have access to large testing environments, eye tracking software, or the ability to do large user studies to gather information. For this project I used a combination or Optimizely for AB testing, Google Analytics to validate my findings, Peek usability testing to get live analysis of users on the site, and my personal favorite, Hotjar to view a users actual journy through the website.
